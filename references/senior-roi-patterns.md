# Senior ROI patterns

A good senior-level interview problem trains a reusable decision, not trivia.

Strong signals:

- The candidate identifies and states an invariant.
- The candidate chooses the right state representation.
- The candidate handles edge cases without special-case sprawl.
- The candidate can compare a straightforward approach with an optimized one.
- The candidate can explain complexity from the maintained state, not from memorized labels.
- Follow-ups naturally explore constraints, trade-offs, or alternative data shapes.

Weak signals:

- The problem is mostly syntax or library recall.
- The problem is a disguised clone of a famous exercise.
- The optimal solution is a trick with little reusable value.
- The tests reward memorizing one pattern name without explaining why it applies.
- The story is elaborate but does not change the algorithmic reasoning.

Good pattern families for this project include hash maps, sliding windows, intervals, stacks,
heaps, graph traversal, topological ordering, binary search on answer, dynamic programming, and
streaming/state-machine problems.
